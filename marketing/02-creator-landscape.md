## What a shoutout actually costs

No devotional page publishes a rate card. Every price must come from a DM. The anchors we have:

- **A 40K art page sells an IG shoutout for ~$20** on Fiverr
  ([listing](https://www.fiverr.com/lineupads/shoutout-your-brand-on-our-20k-art-instagram)) —
  this anchors the low end
- India rate-card benchmarks ([CreatorIQ](https://creatoriq.in/rates/india-guide)): nano
  (1–10K) ₹1,000–11,612/reel; micro (10–50K) ₹1,857–34,834/reel; mid (50–100K)
  ₹8,340–68,108/reel
- Feed posts and stories cost less than reels

**Your $50–100 (₹4,200–8,400) instinct is right** for a feed post or story on a 50K–300K
devotional page. A dedicated reel on the same page will cost more.

### How to run the first buy

1. **Verify first.** Open all ~25 handles in-app, screenshot live follower + recent reel
   view/like counts, compute rough engagement. Run a HypeAuditor or SocialBlade audit on any page
   before paying — bot-inflated followings are common in this niche.
2. **DM the 8–10 most buyable**: @krishnmotivation, @bhagavadgitachanting, @bhag.avad_gita,
   @the.bhagvad.gita, @shri_bhagavad_gita, @art_for_krishna, @krishna_s_d_art,
   @artist_krishna*\_\_, @gitadaily*, @bhagavada.gitaquotes. Ask for their rate and a sample reel.
3. **Pitch it as a gift, not an ad.** "A 100% free, no-ads Gita app and a free Gita GPT" frames
   the promo as devotional service. In this niche that should raise reply rates and lower quotes.
4. **Run a $300–500 price-discovery test** across 5–8 pages: same creative, **unique tracked
   install link per page**, measure cost-per-install, then re-buy only what converts.
5. **Barter where cash does not fit.** ISKCON regional pages and art creators may take app
   features or promotion of their art in exchange for a shoutout. Non-profit-to-non-profit swaps
   are on-brand and free.

Attribution is not optional — without a unique link per page there is no way to tell a good buy
from a bad one, and this is exactly where marketing budgets get quietly wasted.

## Pinterest — yes, still worth it, with realistic expectations

Pinterest is **not** in decline: **631M monthly active users** (Q1 2026), 70% female, Gen Z the
fastest-growing segment at 42% of global users, US the largest market at ~95M
([stats](https://www.getafollower.com/blog/pinterest-user-statistics/)).

What the 2026 algorithm rewards:

- **Fresh pins get a 24–48h visibility boost.** "Fresh" = a unique image + URL + description
  combination; the image is the strongest signal
- **1000×1500 (2:3)** is the correct size; the feed is built for it and crops everything else
- **Video pins get ~2.5× the engagement** of static
- Four ranking signals: Pin quality, Pinner quality, Relevance (keywords), Recency
- Top brands publish **5–10 fresh pins/day**

**The honest caveat:** Pinterest is a slow-compounding SEO play, not a viral one. Traffic builds
over 3–6 months and then keeps paying for years. And its US/female skew only partly overlaps our
India-heavy devotional core — **expect web traffic, not app installs**.

That makes Pinterest a near-perfect fit for **radhakrishna.com**, whose entire job is top-of-funnel
traffic. Pins should deep-link to verse and story pages, with boards per theme ("Gita Verses on
Duty", "Krishna Quotes", "Radha Krishna Aesthetic", "Krishna Wallpapers").
