# 01 — Bhagavad Gita For All: competitor teardown

Research date: 2026-07-25. Every figure below carries its source. Where something could not be
verified it says so rather than guessing — that rule exists because this project has already

## Who they actually are

**Faith Tech Labs Private Limited** (Thane, Maharashtra), a **for-profit** faith-tech company.
Founder/CEO Prithviraaj Shetty (29), CMO Siddhanth Shetty. Incorporated 2013, CIN
U72100MH2013PTC250404. App package `com.bgfa`, iOS `id6504813763`.

**FY2025 revenue: ₹4.48 Cr, up 269% YoY.**
([company registry](https://www.thecompanycheck.com/company/faith-tech-labs-private-limited/U72100MH2013PTC250404))

This matters for one reason: they are a venture-scale commercial operation with a paid
acquisition budget. We are a non-profit that gives everything away. **We cannot out-spend them
and should not try.** Everything below is about what to borrow, not what to match.

## Their model is the mirror image of ours

|               | BGFA                                       | Us                          |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| Price         | 7-day free trial → ~₹79/month subscription | Free, forever               |
| Free content  | Chapter 1 only                             | All 18 chapters, 700 verses |
| Ads           | None, but paywalled                        | None, and not paywalled     |
| Physical book | ₹12,999 coffee-table edition               | —                           |
| Org           | For-profit, ₹4.48 Cr revenue               | Non-profit                  |

Their entire funnel terminates at a subscription. **That is our wedge, and it is honest:** we do
not need to attack them, only to state plainly what we are. Their own weakness supports it —
reviews show English audio is complete only through Chapter 4 (developer-confirmed in review
replies), so "complete and free" is a real contrast, not a slogan.

## Downloads: what is verified and what is not

**The ~400,000-downloads-last-month Sensor Tower figure could not be corroborated.** No public
Sensor Tower, data.ai, or AppMagic page carries it, and no press cites it. It is not necessarily
wrong — those tools paywall app-level estimates — but we should not repeat it as fact.

What _is_ verifiable, from Play's own embedded data object:

- **~1,469,493 lifetime Android installs** (the "10L+" badge rounds this)
- **4.5★ from ~9,220 Play reviews**
- **iOS India: 4.7★, ~1,400 ratings, #1 in Books (Free)**; US iOS only 4.6★ from 61 ratings

Against ~1.47M lifetime installs, 400K in a single month is plausible only as a one-off spike
(the Aug 2025 celebrity launch, or a festival), **not a steady-state rate**. If we want the real
number it needs a paid Sensor Tower pull.

## Paid advertising: the engine behind the "organic" numbers

This is the most important finding in the whole teardown.

- **~270 active Meta ads** on page `396876846839926`, plus **~230 more** on a second page,
  `823941514137240` ("Gita For All App") — **~500 active creatives running simultaneously**
- **55 Google ads** via verified advertiser "Faith Tech Labs Private Limited", spanning YouTube
  video and Display/Discovery
  ([Ads Transparency](https://adstransparency.google.com/?region=anywhere&domain=bhagavadgitaforall.com))
- Always-on, not burst: individual ad start dates spread Mar–Jul 2026, all still active

**Exact spend is not disclosed** — Meta only publishes spend ranges for political/EU ads, and
Google does not publish it at all. The ~500 concurrent creatives are the only signal, and they
indicate a professionalised performance-marketing team, not a founder boosting posts.

**So their 676K Instagram following is a paid-and-organic hybrid.** Their lifetime engagement
rate is **0.21%** (SocialBlade: avg 1,376 likes, 23.7 comments per post) — very low for that
follower count, which is the signature of an audience partly bought rather than earned. Do not
benchmark ourselves against their follower number.

### Their ad playbook, worth copying

1. **Comment-to-DM as the primary CTA.** "Comment 'APP' to watch the full explanation." Drives
   comment volume (a ranking signal), auto-DMs the link, and builds a retargetable audience.
   **This works organically at zero media cost** — the single most copyable tactic here.
2. **Two cheap, endlessly-repeatable video templates:** (a) a UGC talking-head testimonial shot
   at home, (b) a black card with big white hook text plus voiceover. That is how you get to 500
   creatives.
3. **Open loops in the hook:** "Most people think freedom means doing whatever you want. The Gita
   offers a different perspective…" — withhold the payoff behind the app.
4. **Newsjacking in paid**, e.g. a creative tied to the film _Dhurandhar_.
5. **Position against doomscrolling:** "One shloka video a day keeps the anxiety away."

## Social: the shape of it

| Channel                                                                                     | Size                                | Note                                           |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Instagram [@bhagavadgitaforall\_](https://www.instagram.com/bhagavadgitaforall_/)           | ~676K, 1,288 posts                  | Flagship. 0.21% lifetime ER                    |
| YouTube [@BhagavadGitaForAll\_](https://socialblade.com/youtube/handle/bhagavadgitaforall_) | 33.6K subs, 598 videos, 3.27M views | Created Apr 2024, ~1 video/day, ~+100 subs/day |
| Facebook                                                                                    | ~41K                                | Cross-post mirror, heavy hashtag blocks        |
| X/Twitter                                                                                   | none found                          | Their Linktree omits it entirely               |

**The backbone is a numbered daily series:** "Daily Gita Lesson #180" — cinematic AI Krishna /
Vishwarupa imagery, one shloka as on-screen text, fixed bold yellow-white typography.

**But the daily posts are not what grows them.** Typical daily reels do ~40–50K views. The
outliers do the work:

| Post                                             | Views    | Multiple of baseline |
| ------------------------------------------------ | -------- | -------------------- |
| Vidyut Jammwal celebrity collab                  | **9.7M** | ~200×                |
| Testimonial reel ("Why BGFA?", man holding book) | **5.4M** | ~120×                |
| Cinematic Vishwarupa app promo                   | **1.3M** | ~25×                 |

The lesson is not "post daily and you will grow". It is **"post daily to stay alive, and engineer
a handful of spectacle or collab moments to actually grow."**

Their **Story Highlights are a funnel**: Press → Reviews → App Videos → Book → Trailer → App
Features. Social proof first, product last. Cheap to copy.

They use **Indian devotional audio** ("Mahabharat Theme", "Braj Swar"), not Western trending
sounds — correct for the audience and worth matching.

### How they get them

Not Cameo. The pattern — a Mumbai launch event, then near-identical writeups across adgully,
Business News This Week, India Education Diary, Newspatrolling, Indiantelevision — is
**agency-managed brand deals plus paid PR-wire syndication**. One release, six-plus outlets, and
an "as seen in" wall built cheaply.

### What celebrity access actually costs in India

| Tier               | Cost                                                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Bollywood A-lister | **₹5–15 Cr** per endorsement (top names ₹1.5–7 Cr _per shoot day_) |
| Cricketer          | **₹2–10 Cr/year**                                                  |
| Agency commission  | +10–20% on top                                                     |

([Tring 2026 rate list](https://www.tring.co.in/brands/celebrity-endorsement-price-list))

**Cameo is not a route to Indian A-listers.** Global Cameo videos run $5–$999, and Cameo has
publicly said Indian cricketers and Bollywood are verticals it does _not_ yet offer. Indian
equivalents exist — [Tring](https://www.tring.co.in), [Rapid Kings](https://rapidkings.com) —
but per-talent rates need a quote.

**Recommendation:** skip A-listers entirely. A non-profit has one thing a ₹4.48 Cr for-profit
cannot buy — it can credibly ask for a **gift of service**. Target (a) public figures who already
post about the Gita or bhakti, (b) retired cricketers and character actors, (c) mythology
micro-creators like the child storyteller BGFA uses, who cost a fraction and often convert
better. Ask for a free, reverent collaboration; offer a co-branded Gita GPT moment.

## ASO, and their book trick

- Title: **"Bhagavad Gita For All | Video"**, subtitle **"World's Only Video Gita"** — they escaped
  the crowded "Bhagavad Gita" head term by inventing a category they own
- Description keyword-stuffed across Roman _and_ Devanagari intent: "Bhagwat Geeta", "Geeta
  Updesh", "Gita Shloka", "Krishna Gita"
- **Weekly-ish update cadence** (v7.1 shipped within days of capture) keeps the freshness signal
- In-app retention: "Shloka of the Day", gamified badges and leaderboards, and **"My Krishna
  Chat"** AI (Feb 2026) explicitly aimed at Gen Z

**The book is the cleverest thing they do.** A ₹12,999 coffee-table hardcover where **every shloka
carries a QR code** linking to that verse's video in the app, and buyers get lifetime access. It
turns an offline object into an install engine and a retention lock.

**We can beat this on distribution rather than production value:** a cheap or temple-distributed
free pocket Gita with per-verse QR codes deep-linking into our app costs a fraction and reaches
far more people. They charge ₹12,999; we can give it away.

## What to borrow, ranked

1. **Comment-to-DM CTA** — works organically, zero cost, highest-volume tactic they run
2. **Numbered daily series** with a rigid template, so daily production is assembly not invention
3. **Second-person question hooks**, scripture as the payoff not the opener
4. **Story Highlights as a funnel** — Press/Reviews → App → GitaGPT → Download
5. **QR-per-verse printed Gita**, given away rather than sold at ₹12,999
6. **PR-wire syndication** for our own milestones and festivals
7. **ASO**: an ownable subtitle ("Free, ad-free Gita + AI"), full Roman+Devanagari keyword set,
   fortnightly update cadence
8. **A few engineered spectacle pieces** per quarter — their non-celebrity ceiling was a 1.3M
   Vishwarupa reel

## What not to copy

- **"India's #1" / "1M+ downloads" style claims.** These appear only in their own ad copy. Our
  entire differentiation is that we do not inflate.
- **Their engagement-rate problem.** A big bought following with 0.21% ER is not a goal.
- **Aggressive celebrity spend.** Crore-level fees against a non-profit budget, and a devotee
  audience may read paid star power on scripture as cheapening it.
- **Paywalling scripture.** Obviously.

## Open questions for a human

- Real ad spend needs a paid tool (Sensor Tower ad intelligence, SimilarWeb, Foreplay) — $100–1,000+/mo. Worth it only if we start competing on paid.
- Are their celebrity deals paid endorsements, or equity/advisor arrangements? Suniel Shetty is a known wellness investor. Not disclosed publicly.
- Does the Foundation have _any_ appetite for paid talent, or does that conflict with the no-sponsorship stance?
- Would a devotee audience react badly to us running celebrity promotion at all? Worth sentiment-testing before committing budget.
